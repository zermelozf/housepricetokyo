A Data-Driven Approach to Tokyo Real Estate

Buying a house in Tokyo can be a daunting task, especially for foreigners. In this series of articles, I'll guide you through the process using data and machine learning to make informed decisions. This first part focuses on understanding how property values are determined in Tokyo.

Key Factors Affecting Property Values

Location, Location, Location

As in any major city, location is the most significant factor in determining property values. Our analysis of MLIT data shows that properties in central Tokyo can be worth up to 150% more than similar properties in outer areas.

Building Type and Depreciation

One of the most interesting findings from our analysis is how different building materials depreciate over time. Using MLIT data, we were able to model the depreciation curves for wooden, concrete, and steel structures.

As you can see, wooden structures depreciate much faster than concrete or steel buildings. This is an important consideration when evaluating older properties.

Land Characteristics

The characteristics of the land itself also play a significant role in property values. Our analysis shows that:

  • Larger plots command a premium, but the value per square meter decreases as size increases
  • South-facing properties are generally more valuable due to better sunlight exposure
  • Irregularly shaped plots can be significantly less valuable than regular rectangular plots
